# Mental Health and Wellbeing

## Signs You May Need Support

Students should consider reaching out for help if they experience:

* Persistent low mood lasting more than two weeks.
* Loss of interest in activities they previously enjoyed.
* Difficulty concentrating on studies or daily tasks.
* Changes in sleep patterns — sleeping too much or too little.
* Withdrawing from friends, family, or social activities.
* Feeling hopeless or that things will never improve.

## Coping Strategies

### Daily habits
* Maintain a regular sleep schedule.
* Eat balanced meals and stay hydrated.
* Exercise for at least 20 minutes most days.
* Limit social media if it increases stress or comparison.

### Social support
* Talk to a trusted friend, family member, or roommate.
* Join a student club or support group.
* Attend university wellbeing workshops.

### Academic balance
* Set realistic goals for each study session.
* Use the Pomodoro technique: 25 minutes study, 5 minutes break.
* Ask lecturers or tutors for extensions if genuinely overwhelmed.

## Professional Support at MMU

The MMU Counselling Unit offers free confidential support for enrolled students. Appointments can be made through the student portal or by visiting the counselling office on campus.

## Peer Support

Sometimes talking to someone who understands student life helps. Consider:
* Peer support programmes on campus.
* Befrienders Malaysia for emotional support.
* Talian HEAL 15555 for mental health guidance.

Source: MMU Counselling Services and Malaysian mental health resources.
