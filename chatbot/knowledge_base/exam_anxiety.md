# Exam Anxiety Support

## Understanding Exam Anxiety

Exam anxiety is a common experience among university students. Physical symptoms may include rapid heartbeat, sweating, nausea, or difficulty sleeping. Mental symptoms include racing thoughts, blanking out during exams, and excessive worry about failure.

## Before the Exam

* Review your notes regularly rather than cramming the night before.
* Create a realistic revision timetable with short breaks.
* Practice past-year questions under timed conditions.
* Get 7–8 hours of sleep the night before.
* Avoid excessive caffeine — it can increase anxiety.

## During the Exam

* Read all questions carefully before answering.
* Start with questions you feel confident about.
* Use the 4-7-8 breathing technique if you feel panicked: inhale 4 seconds, hold 7, exhale 8.
* If your mind goes blank, pause, breathe, and re-read the question slowly.

## After the Exam

* Avoid comparing answers with classmates immediately.
* Reflect on what went well, not only what went wrong.
* Reward yourself for completing the exam regardless of outcome.

## When to Seek Help

Contact the university counselling centre if exam anxiety:
* Prevents you from studying effectively for weeks.
* Causes panic attacks or physical illness.
* Leads to avoidance of exams or classes entirely.

Source: MMU Student Counselling Unit.
