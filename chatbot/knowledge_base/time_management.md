# Time Management for Students

## Common Time Management Problems

* Procrastination until deadlines.
* Underestimating how long tasks take.
* Trying to multitask on assignments and social media.
* Not prioritising urgent vs important tasks.

## The Eisenhower Matrix

| | Urgent | Not Urgent |
|---|--------|------------|
| **Important** | Do first (exams tomorrow) | Schedule (long-term projects) |
| **Not Important** | Delegate or limit | Eliminate (excessive scrolling) |

## Weekly Planning Steps

1. List all deadlines and exam dates for the semester.
2. Break each assignment into smaller tasks with mini-deadlines.
3. Block fixed times for lectures, meals, and rest.
4. Leave buffer time — tasks often take 1.5× longer than expected.
5. Review your plan every Sunday evening.

## Tools That Help

* Google Calendar or Notion for scheduling.
* To-do lists with maximum 3 priority tasks per day.
* Study groups for accountability.

## When Stress Becomes Overwhelming

If time pressure causes constant anxiety, sleep loss, or inability to function, speak to a counsellor. Time management coaching is available through the MMU Counselling Unit.
